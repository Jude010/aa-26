# Autonomous Agents Assignment 2026

**Autonomous, Anthropomorphic, Lifeform**

![Life Jim](life.jpg)

Create a 2D, 3D or holographic artificial lifeform that can interact with its surroundings and the player. A virtual creature with a mind of its own that can autonomously achieve tasks and goals based on its needs. It can have big round eyes and make cute sounds. Manipulate the user by whatever means to believe your lifeform is actually "alive" — that it has thoughts and emotions of its own, however mysterious and artificial they may be. Use steering behaviors, procedural animations, inverse kinematics, FSM architecture, Behavior Trees or an LLM to be the brain of the lifeform. Give the lifeform a name, identity and personality.

You can fork [this repo](https://github.com/skooter500/miniature-rotary-phone) to get lots of steering behaviour and procedural animations, or [this one](https://github.com/skooter500/hands) for XR hand tracking with autonomous behaviors.

You can create all assets in Godot using CSG nodes or Blender.

## Marking Scheme

### 1. Groovyness — Visuals & Sound, Quality of Outcome

| Grade | Indicative Aspects |
|-------|-------------------|
| **1** | Project looks exceptional with a high level of polish on visuals and sound. Outstanding use of colour, form and visual style — this can be programmery! Great use of visual effects: particle systems, post-processing or custom shaders. Real-time movement and animation. Highly artistic interpretation conveying a convincing sense of life. Sound design is purposeful and enhances immersion. Deployed and running on a device. Holographic or XR elements present. |
| **2.1** | Attempts to model the brief convincingly but has some glitches. Looks pretty good with decent models. Limited or no custom visual effects — perhaps a particle system. Sound effects sourced online and used appropriately. Some interactivity and button presses. Less coherent visual style but good novelty value. Running in editor or on PC. |
| **2.2** | One or two models imported from online sources. No reference material evident. Little or no interactivity — mostly an animated visual. Perhaps relying heavily on starter code. Little or no sound. Running in editor. |
| **Pass** | Model from an online source or a primitive shape that does little or nothing. |
| **Fail** | Does not compile or little/no content. |

---

### 2. Complexity — Coding, Maths, Algorithm & System Design

| Grade | Indicative Aspects |
|-------|-------------------|
| **1** | 15–20 hours of work evident. A sophisticated system demonstrating strong self-directed learning and confident use of techniques from the course and beyond. 5 or 6 new, well-designed classes that interact cohesively to implement system functionality. Code is cleanly separated into appropriate methods and classes following [SOLID principles](https://www.digitalocean.com/community/conceptual-articles/s-o-l-i-d-the-first-five-principles-of-object-oriented-design). Several hundred lines of self-written code. Complex algorithms present — generative systems, path finding, procedural animation, Boids, inverse kinematics, Behavior Trees, FSM or LLM integration. Advanced use of Godot systems including animation, shaders and particle systems. Gizmos on all nodes for debugging. Deployed and demoed on a device. |
| **2.1** | 10–15 hours of work evident. A less complex but functional system with meaningful interactivity. Several self-written classes; some code adapted from tutorials or online sources with clear understanding demonstrated. Uses techniques from the course such as transform manipulation and Boids. Around 100 lines of self-written code. Some adherence to SOLID principles. No custom gizmos. Long methods present. Running in editor on PC. |
| **2.2** | 5–10 hours of work evident. A simple system with one or two scripts based on course examples. Most functionality derived from tutorials with little customisation or evidence of understanding. Little interactivity. No use of broader Godot systems. Random colours, no audio or inappropriate audio. Works in editor but not tested as a build or on device. |
| **Pass** | Under 5 hours of work. A single script or entirely tutorial-derived. Compiles and runs. |
| **Fail** | No submission or nothing works. |

---

### 3. Project Management & Documentation

| Grade | Indicative Aspects |
|-------|-------------------|
| **1** | 30–40 commits. Feature branches used throughout. For team projects: equal distribution of commits across members clearly visible. All commits commented meaningfully. All sections of the README template completed. Document includes genuine reflective elements — *What did I learn?* Sources properly referenced. Self-directed learning beyond the course material is evident and discussed. Embedded, public, YouTube video (do **not** check "Made for Kids"). Video is made from a **build** (not the editor) and demonstrates all features. Video includes a explains the project. Source/reference material included in the repo. |
| **2.1** | 20–30 commits. One or two branches. All template sections filled out. Sources referenced. Source material in repo. Some reflective content. Minor issues with the video. For team projects, reasonable but not fully equal commit distribution. |
| **2.2** | 10–20 commits, terse or no comments. No branches. Documentation incomplete. Little evidence of reflective learning. Missing references. Issues with the video. |
| **Pass** | Under 10 commits. Terse or no comments. No branches. Documentation incomplete. No video. |
| **Fail** | No submission. |
